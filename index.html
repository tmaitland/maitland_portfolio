A little about me

I have spent most of my life with my face in a book, in front of a computer screen, in a sketchpad--drawing, or in a notebook--writing. Despite this, I have managed to find myself outdoors to enjoy nature and interact with people. I consider myself to be an intellectual and I enjoy fields of study where the learning possibilities are endless--hence my pursuit of web development. I am also an empath, and I often strive to ensure that everyone is realistically content with their current circumstances. I am also willing to help others resolve any conflicts or fix inefficiencies.

I've got skills

Web Developer

As a teen, mySpace, blogs, and forums were the extent of my exposure to coding. I didn't know I could pursue coding as a career path until my last year of university. Since then, I have built my own website from scatch after taking a Web Design Principles course at the University of Florida. Most recently, I have learned to build small web applications thanks to the instructors at Florida Vocational Institute's coding school, TechLaunch.

Historian

Historians use a scientific process to gather information in order to make sense of people's actions and the subsequent consequences. It only made sense for me to be drawn to some sort of science, especially technological science. I worked at UF's Samuel Proctor Oral History Program as a Transcriber and Student Assistant for almost two years. While there, I often helped digitize historical accounts for online and print use. Historians need the web to preserve history and improve its accessibility to anyone, anywhere, at any time.

Writer

I have been writing all my life. I wrote poems, stories, journals, and of course papers throughout my entire school career. Now I also write code and it's fun for me. It's exciting to elicit emotions, thoughts, and supply knowledge with words alone, which is what I have done as a writer. As an author of code, or a programmer, I can also do this with websites and the programs I build and run.
